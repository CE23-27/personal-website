Measurement and Inference (Experimental Physics)

  • Estimator Bias in Low-Count Poisson Fitting — A Muon Lifetime Measurement

    Diagnosed and corrected a systematic bias in the standard χ² histogram fit — demonstrated via bin-width dependence — and used a binned Poisson maximum-likelihood estimator to measure the cosmic-ray muon lifetime and extract the Fermi coupling constant.

  • Model Comparison and Misspecification — Single-Photon Double-Slit Interference

    Compared two competing diffraction models — far-field Fraunhofer against a finite-distance path-integral model — and discriminated them quantitatively: a dimensionless Fresnel parameter predicted where they would diverge, and a slit-width consistency check exposed the model that absorbed its misspecification into an unphysical parameter. Demonstrated on Young's double-slit in the single-photon regime.

  • Cancelling the Dominant Systematic — Optical Pumping of Rubidium

    Determined the Landé g-factors of both rubidium isotopes by optical pumping and RF spectroscopy. Their ratio cancels the dominant coil-calibration systematic exactly, isolating a real ~3σ deviation from theory that the per-isotope uncertainty would otherwise have buried — backed by a data-driven recalibration of the apparatus's field coils.
